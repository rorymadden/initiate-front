Benefits
For the Association and its Members

Associations are the go-to leader for industry insights and thought leadership. Conferences allow an association to inform their members on the latest challenges, changes and trends in their industry as well as providing invaluable networking and marketing opportunities. Keynote conferences provide exposure, member engagement and key revenue for many associations, along with new opportunities to increase the membership base.

Insight & Leadership

We research with both advocates of your brand and those outside of your existing network to ensure the event maximises your exposure to a broader market. We analyse where there is growth, what is the next big thing to affect your market, who are the market disruptors and how the audience can gain the most value.

Experience Design & Networking

Creating an event provides associations with an unrivalled opportunity to connect with members and the wider industry in a live environment. It also puts associations at the forefront of facilitating business opportunities and gathering of colleagues to unite in an otherwise divided industry.

Networking is a key activity that is not only fun, but critical to member growth and business development. Many attendees feel the best learning experience at a conference often stems from an interaction with a colleague or peer and not necessarily from on stage. For an association, it means you are facilitating these key business opportunities, building relationships and taking action.

Ongoing Communication

Associations have a critical and serious role to deliver for the betterment of their industry. However, along with agendas like regulatory updates, it is often appropriate to ‘connect’ with the industry by providing insights into movers and shakers, news, updates and more.

Launching an event means that not only do you get an annual showcase and experience, you get on-going content produced from pre-and post event communication with a wider market. Whether it may be through interviews, insights into a case study, webinars, or post show presentations. Whatever the format, developing a conference provides you with a relevant talking point for almost 9 months of the year, demonstrating the leadership of the association within its marketplace.

Who We Are
Acountability. Integrity. Transparency

Initiate Network was founded by a team of individuals who believe in the value and vital role associations play in their industry. Our founders include members that have worked on both the commercial and association side of conference organisations. Together, the team at Initiate have delivered over 100 events ranging in size from 50 to 22,000 attendees and have combined a stellar events process that is unbeatable in the marketplace. We created Initiate with the belief that we could help associations not only provide thought-leadership but to better connect with their industry. Conferences are a critical way to bring the industry together, but also can be a key revenue driver to fund mainstream association activities.

Many associations understand the role a larger conference has to play but often lack the resources to deliver a non-core business activity. Other associations may ‘endorse’ an event from an independent conference organisation by marketing the event to members in return for branding. Initiate started because we believe that neither associations nor their industry were gaining long term benefits from these events and believed there was a better model for associations.

We understand the detail needed to create a commercial conference, coupled with the unique needs of an association, including membership initiatives, regulatory and communications. We see our partnership model as offering better benefits for the association allowing us to build a more sustainable and long term relationship. This alleviates risk, maximises revenue and delivers vital exposure to new and existing association members.

We do everything we can to ensure that associations do not have to pay upfront costs, or deviate from their core regulatory or other objectives.
